# Monitor AI Security with Defender for Cloud

Your CISO wants a status report on the AI workload protections deployed this week: which AI resources are protected, what has Defender for Cloud detected, and what needs to be remediated first.

This environment has been pre-configured with an Azure AI Foundry endpoint, Defender for AI Services enabled, and a set of pre-generated adversarial queries that have run against the endpoint. Those queries were not blocked — this endpoint has no guardrails, by design — so Defender for AI has had behavioral data to analyze. Your job is to read and interpret what it found.

In this lab, you will:

- Review AI workload protection status in Defender for Cloud
- Identify active security findings and assess their severity
- Review AI security recommendations
- Briefly consider the findings in context

This exercise should take approximately **15** minutes to complete.

> **Note**: The findings in this environment were generated by synthetic adversarial traffic run during environment setup. In a production environment, these findings would come from real attacker activity against unprotected AI endpoints. The finding types — jailbreak attempts, prompt injection probes, and anomalous volume — are the same in both cases.

---

## Open the Data and AI security dashboard

1. Sign in to the [Azure portal](https://portal.azure.com) using your **Global Administrator** credentials.

1. In the search bar, search for and select **Microsoft Defender for Cloud**.

1. In the left menu, select **Data and AI security**.

    > **Note**: If you do not see **Data and AI security** in the left menu, look under **Cloud security** or use the Defender for Cloud search bar. The menu label may vary slightly by portal version.

1. On the **Data and AI security** overview page, review the protection status summary. Identify how many AI resources are listed as protected versus unprotected or partially covered.

1. Locate **sc500-lab3d-foundry** in the protected resources list and confirm its Defender for AI Services status shows as **Protected**.

---

## Review active security findings

1. In the **Data and AI security** section, select **Findings** (or navigate to the **Alerts** view filtered to AI workloads).

1. Review the active findings list. Note the total count, the severity levels represented, and which resource each finding targets.

1. Select the highest-severity finding to open the finding detail view.

1. In the finding detail view, record the following:

    | Field | Value |
    |-------|-------|
    | **Finding title** | (the name of the detection) |
    | **Severity** | High / Medium / Low |
    | **Affected resource** | sc500-lab3d-foundry |
    | **Trigger description** | (what Defender for AI detected) |
    | **Recommended action** | (what the remediation step is) |

1. Return to the findings list and review whether additional findings are present for different attack types — for example, a prompt injection finding distinct from a jailbreak finding.

    > **Note**: The pre-generated traffic included jailbreak attempts, prompt injection probes, and high-volume burst queries. Not all three may have generated separate findings — Defender for AI correlates related signals into finding groups rather than creating one alert per query. You may see one to three findings depending on how the behavioral analysis grouped the traffic.

---

## Review AI security recommendations

1. In the left menu, navigate to **Recommendations** and filter the recommendations list to show only AI-related recommendations, or search for **AI**.

1. Identify at least one recommendation that applies to **sc500-lab3d-foundry**.

1. Select the recommendation to open its detail view. Review:

    - The **security control** this recommendation falls under
    - The **remediation steps** described in the recommendation detail
    - The **severity** and **freshness** of the recommendation

1. Notice that at least one recommendation is likely related to the absence of a content filter on **sc500-lab3d-foundry**. This is intentional — the endpoint has no guardrails so that the adversarial traffic could generate detectable findings. The recommendation is correct: a content safety guardrail should be applied. (You configured one in Lab 3C; this environment is separate.)

## Consider the findings in context

Before you leave this lab, take a moment to consider what you are looking at.

The findings on this screen came from an unprotected endpoint that received adversarial queries during environment setup. Defender for AI detected the attack patterns — not by analyzing individual blocked requests, but by identifying behavioral sequences across multiple queries. That is the distinction between a guardrail (which blocks one request at a time) and a behavioral detection system (which identifies a campaign across many requests).

In a production environment, this dashboard would show findings from your real AI workloads — the Foundry endpoints, Copilot agents, and AI services your organization has deployed. The security engineer's job is to review these findings, prioritize by severity and blast radius, and escalate the highest-risk findings for immediate remediation.

Consider: looking at the findings in front of you, what is the single highest-severity finding, and what one action would reduce the most risk for **sc500-lab3d-foundry**?

You do not need to write a formal answer. Just have an answer in mind before you close this lab — that is the habit this lab is designed to reinforce.
